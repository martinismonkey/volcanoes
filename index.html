How volcanoes are formed

Volcanoes are usually formed when 2 tectonic plates
collide with eachother.

Shield Volcanoes

Shield volcanoes don't pop out of the ground much and are known for looking
like a warriors shield lying on the ground. It produces lots of lava.

Stratovolcanoes

Composite volcanoes/ Stratovolcanoes are very tall volcanes.
They are usually thousands of feet or meters tall. You'll
notice that they don't start off very steep but they
get steeper near the end.

Ciner Cone Volcanoes

Cinder cone volcanoes are the most common type of volcano in the world.
They look just like the volcanoes you would imagine because they are very
steep and have a hole at the top.
